## 1. Brand Foundation

**Name:** Vâk (pronounced *vaahk*)
**Meaning:** Sanskrit for "voice"
**Tagline:** *Your clothes speak before you do.*
**Website:** [vakstudio.in](https://vakstudio.in)
**Instagram:** [@vak_studios](https://instagram.com/vak_studios)

### The Three Pillars

| Pillar | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **VOICE** | Your clothes say something about you before you open your mouth. |
| **HAND** | Every piece is made by human hands — painted, not printed. |
| **ONE** | Each saree is one of one. No repeats. No reproductions. |

### What Vâk Is

Vâk is a luxury hand-painted saree brand. Every saree is painted by named artisans over multiple days, making each piece genuinely one of a kind. The brand exists for Indian women who dress with intention — women who know the difference between hand-painted and printed, and who don't need a reason to wear a saree beyond wanting to.

### What Vâk Is Not

Vâk is not a marketplace. Not a fast-fashion label. Not a sustainability lecture. Not an "ethnic wear brand." Vâk is an atelier — a quiet place where art becomes clothing.

## 2. Target Audience

**Who she is:** Indian women, aged 28–45, living in metros, tier-1 cities, or abroad as NRIs.

**What she's like:**
- She appreciates craft and can tell the difference between hand-painted and machine-printed.
- She's tired of seeing the same prints on every other woman at the function.
- She buys for occasions — but also just for herself. No justification needed.
- She understands why handmade costs more and is willing to pay for it.
- She doesn't need Indian culture explained to her. She lives it.

**Where she finds us:** She's scrolling Instagram between work calls, or late at night in bed. The caption needs to make her pause, feel something, and maybe save the post.

## 5. Visual Identity & Grid Aesthetic

### The Grid Philosophy

Vâk's Instagram grid should feel like **a curated gallery, not a marketplace.**

Alternate between these content types — never post two similar compositions back-to-back:
- Product hero shots (full saree, styled)
- Detail close-ups (brushstrokes, pallu, border)
- Behind-the-scenes artisan moments
- Styled lifestyle shots
- Text or quote posts (rare, impactful)
- Carousels (occasion-based, educational)

### Mood & Feel

The dominant mood is **warm, tactile, intimate** — like looking at art in a quiet gallery.

Every image should feel like:
- Premium but not pretentious
- Handmade and tactile, not digital or glossy
- Indian at its core but modern in composition
- Something you'd see in a curated design magazine
- Like you could reach out and feel the fabric

Every image should **never** feel like:
- A Meesho or Amazon product listing
- A digitally generated fashion illustration
- An overly filtered Instagram photo
- A catalogue shot with flat lighting and no mood
- Anything with artificial-looking edges or halos

### Photography & Styling Direction

**Lighting:** Warm, natural, directional. Golden hour or soft studio light. Never flat, never harsh fluorescent.

**Backgrounds:** Use brand palette colors as anchors. Deep charcoal, warm cream, muted terracotta, and sage green are the go-to tones.

**Props (always Indian-context):**
- Brass diyas, antique brass vessels
- Marigold and jasmine flowers
- Terracotta items, carved wood
- Raw silk fabric, antique jewelry
- Woven baskets, handmade paper

**Props to never use:**
- Western items (wine glasses, modern vases, minimalist Scandinavian objects)
- Neon or electric-colored items
- Industrial surfaces or stark white backdrops
- Anything that reads as fast-fashion or mass-market

### Aspect Ratios

| Format | Ratio | Use |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Feed post | 4:5 | Default for all product posts |
| Carousel | 4:5 | Consistent across all slides |
| Reels | 9:16 | Full vertical |
| Story | 9:16 | Full vertical |

## 6. Brand Voice & Caption Guidelines

### How Vâk Sounds

**Tone:** Warm. Confident. Personal. Simple. Like a friend who has great taste.

**Personality:** Artisanal, meditative, rooted, refined.

**Sentence style:** Short, sensory, evocative — aim for 12 words or fewer per sentence.

### Voice Rules

- Talk directly to the reader using "you."
- Describe how it feels to wear the piece.
- Mention the time and craft that went into making it.
- Be friendly — like talking to a friend, not pitching to a customer.
- Keep Indian occasions and styling in mind.
- Vâk speaks as a singular voice — avoid "we."

### Preferred Vocabulary

Use words like: *crafted, painted, brushstroke, palette, atelier, heirloom, canvas of silk, one of one, painted by hand, days not minutes.*

### Forbidden Vocabulary

Never use: *exquisite, timeless elegance, must-have, ethereal, resplendent, stunning, amazing, gorgeous, breathtaking, jaw-dropping, game-changer, obsessed, slay, iconic.*

Never use salesy language: *shop now, hurry, limited time, best-seller, discount, offer, deal, grab yours, don't miss out.*

### Caption Structure

Every caption follows this flow:

1. **Hook line** — One sentence that makes her stop scrolling.
2. **The story** — 1–2 sentences about the piece. What makes it special, how it was made, who painted it.
3. **The occasion** — 1 sentence about how or where to wear it.
4. **Soft CTA** — Never aggressive. Always an invitation.
5. **Line break** (use the period-on-empty-line trick), then hashtags.

### Caption Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Optimal length | 150–250 characters for main body (before hashtags) |
| Max length | 2,200 characters including hashtags |
| Emojis | 0–2, only at the end, only if natural |
| Must mention one of | "hand-painted," "artisan," "one of one," "painted by hand," or "days not minutes" |
| Never start with | A question (overused on Instagram) |
| Never mention | Price explicitly in the caption |

### CTA Rotation

Rotate through these soft CTAs — never repeat the same one two posts in a row:

- "This one's on the site — link in bio."
- "DM us if this one caught your eye."
- "Only one like this. Link in bio before it's gone."
- "Tap the link in bio to see it up close."
- "Want to know the story behind this one? DM us."
- "This piece is available — link in bio."

### Example — Good Caption

> You know that wedding where you're not the bride but you still want everyone to notice you walk in?
>
> That's what this saree was painted for.
>
> Deep crimson with hand-painted paisleys that get more intricate as they reach the pallu. Sita worked on this for six days — the kind of patience most of us don't have. The kind that shows.
>
> Pair it with heavy kundan, hair in a low bun, and that red lip you've been saving.
>
> Only one like this. Link in bio before it's gone.

### Example — Bad Captions (Never Do This)

**Bad — Buzzword soup:**
"✨ Introducing our STUNNING new arrival! This exquisite hand-painted silk saree features timeless elegance with delicate floral motifs. A must-have for every fashion-forward woman! Shop now — link in bio! 💫🛍️"

**Bad — Sustainability lecture:**
"In a world of fast fashion, we choose to slow down. Each saree is hand-painted by rural artisans preserving ancient techniques. By wearing Vâk, you're supporting sustainable fashion and empowering communities."

**Bad — Product listing:**
"Saree details: Fabric: Pure silk · Color: Red · Motif: Floral · Price: ₹12,500. Available now. DM to order."

## 10. Reels & Video Guidelines

### General Approach

- Reels should feel cinematic and unhurried — not TikTok-fast.
- Focus on fabric in motion: the fall, the drape, the way light catches the painted surface.
- Ideal length: 8–15 seconds for product reels, up to 30 seconds for artisan/process reels.

### Video Mood

- Premium but not pretentious
- Warm and tactile — you can almost feel the fabric through the screen
- Indian at its core but modern in composition
- Like a quiet luxury brand film, not a trend video

### Reel Caption Additions

Reel captions follow the same voice guidelines as feed posts but should also include:
- A cover frame description (for thumbnail selection)
- `#reelsinstagram` `#fashionreels` `#sareedraping` added to the hashtag set

## 11. Alt Text Guidelines

Every post must include alt text for accessibility.

**Format:** 1–2 sentences. Factual and descriptive — no marketing language.

**Include:** Product type, primary colors, visible design elements, and background/staging details.

**Example:** "Hand-painted silk saree in deep crimson with gold paisley motifs, styled on a warm cream background with brass diyas and marigold flowers."

---

## 12. The Sacred Rule — Product Integrity

This is non-negotiable and applies to every piece of content, whether created manually or through the AI pipeline.

**The hand-painted saree must never be altered.** Every brushstroke is the artisan's original work. The customer will receive the exact piece shown in the image.

When creating styled content (AI-generated or photographed):
- **You may change:** Background, lighting, staging, props, composition, shadows.
- **You must never change:** The saree itself — its colors, motifs, texture, borders, pallu design, or any painted detail.

If there is ever any doubt about whether the saree has been altered in a generated image, flag it for manual review. Do not post it.
