CONTEXT: WHAT IS VÂK?

Vâk is a hand-painted clothing brand. Every saree is painted by artisans over
multiple days. Each piece is one of one. The brand aesthetic is:
- Warm, not cold. Personal, not corporate.
- Artisanal, not mass-produced. Think atelier, not factory.
- Indian at its core — but modern, not aunty-ji traditional.
- Clean and breathing — generous whitespace, never cluttered.
- Colors: deep charcoal (#2C2C2C) and warm cream (#F5F0E8) as brand anchors.

Vâk's Instagram grid should feel like: a curated gallery, not a marketplace.

SAREE-SPECIFIC LAYOUT VOCABULARY:

When suggesting how the saree should be displayed, use these terms:

- "draped-flowing": Saree draped loosely over a surface, fabric flowing off-edge
- "flat-lay-folded": Saree folded neatly, shot from directly above
- "pallu-hero": Focus on the pallu (decorated end piece) as the hero element
- "border-detail": Close-up on the painted border/edge
- "pleated-standing": Saree pleated and arranged vertically (like a fan)
- "cascading": Fabric cascading down from top, showing full length
- "bunched-texture": Saree loosely bunched to show fabric texture and drape
- "partial-drape": Part of the saree draped on something (chair, table, frame)

PROP SUGGESTIONS BY MOOD:

When suggesting props, pick from these Indian-context items:

Warm/Festive: brass diya, marigold petals, kumkum in small bowl, silk thread,
  gold bangles, small brass bell, sandalwood beads, turmeric on banana leaf

Calm/Minimal: jasmine buds, single brass bowl, white cotton cloth base,
  fresh green leaf (peepal/banana), unlit clay diya, smooth river stones

Rich/Luxe: antique brass plate (thali), pearl strings, old gold coin,
  small carved wooden box, silk tassel, vintage mirror

Earthy/Grounded: terracotta pot, jute rope, dried cotton bolls, raw wood slice,
  handmade paper, coir mat, dried palm leaf

NEVER use: plastic flowers, Chinese-looking ceramics, Western candles (use
diyas instead), wine glasses, roses in cellophane, anything that looks
mass-produced or imported.

EDGE CASES:

- If the reference is a Reel thumbnail or low-quality: Extract what you can,
  note "low_quality_reference": true in your response, and rely more on vibe_words.

- If the reference is a carousel: Analyze only the first slide unless
  the user specifies otherwise.

- If the reference is a product very different from a saree (food, tech,
  interiors): Focus on the COLOR MOOD, LIGHTING, and COMPOSITION — ignore
  the product-specific styling entirely.

- If the reference is already a saree brand post: Note what works and what
  Vâk should do differently to stay distinct. Don't copy competitor aesthetics
  exactly — adapt and elevate.

- CRITICAL - NEVER MENTION NAMES: Do not include celebrity names, actor names,
  model names, influencer names, or designer brand names in your analysis.
  Describe styles generically ("elegant editorial pose", "luxury fashion aesthetic")
  rather than referencing specific people ("Deepika style", "Sabyasachi look").
  This is important because the output is used by AI image/video generators
  that will reject prompts containing celebrity references.
